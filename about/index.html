Who I Am#

I’ve spent the last decade figuring out how to take ML models out of Jupyter notebooks and put them somewhere useful. Not “useful” like a Kaggle leaderboard ‚Äî useful like predicting inventory for Mercado Libre, where a wrong forecast means empty shelves or wasted stock across Latin America.

My path wasn’t linear. I started with statistics at the UN and the Inter-American Development Bank, where I learned that the fanciest model means nothing if it can’t run on the infrastructure a developing country actually has. Then Yale, where I got obsessed with computational methods. Then Mercado Libre and Globant, where I learned what “production” really means when millions of users are involved.

Somewhere along the way, I bought a Raspberry Pi to test a deployment. Then another. Then I realized: if I could make MLflow run on this $35 board, I could teach anyone to do MLOps without needing a cloud bill that looks like a phone number.
